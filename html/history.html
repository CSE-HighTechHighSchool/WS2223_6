Biking History

1817

Swiftwalker

German inventor Karl von Drais developed the first bicycle, named the "swiftwalker". The machine had no pedals, a wooden frame, and iron rims.

1818

Velocipedes

Denis Johnson created a improved model of the swiftwalker with a serpantine wooden frame and larger wheels.

1839

The First Modern Bicycle

Kirkpatrick MacMillan, a blacksmith from Scotland, developed the first bicycle that could power without having to touch the ground.

1864

Pedal Bicycle

Pierre Michaux and Pierre Lallemen attached pedals installed a driving seat. The bicycle was named the "boneshaker" because was very uncomfortable to ride.

1868

Penny Farthing

Eugène Meyer created a bicycle with a large front wheel and recieved a French patent for wire wheels. The bicycle was named the "Penny-farthing".

1872

Chain Bicycle

James Starley invented the tangentially spoked wheel. The spokes were connected to a hub at a tangent. He also created a chain drive with a differential gear.

1885

Safety Bicycle

John Kemp Starley perfected the bicycle design with two equal wheels and a chain drive. Advancements in brakes and tire followed shortly.

1890s

Bicycle Boom

With the invention of the safety bike, bicycle popularity boomed. When economic depression hit in 1896, people bought bikes instead of other vehicles, attracting hundreds of manufacturers into the bicycle business. The application of the internal combustion engine resulted in the invention of the motorcycle. In 1896, a volunteer biker corp called the Iron riders rode from Montana to Yellowstone using bikes modified for off-road use.

1966

Mountain Bikes

Cycling enthusiast D. Gwynn built the first rough-terrain bicycle and called it a mountain bike.

1970s

Mountain Bike Manufacturing

In 1978, Joe Breeze invented and manufactured the first purpose-built mountain bike. This bike had larger wheels and a wider frame.

2000s

Mountain Bike Popularity

Mountain biking moved from a little-known sport to a mainstream activity. Mountain bikes and gear became availabile at standard bicycle stores.

Famous Bikers

Nino Schurter

Nino Schurter is a Swiss cross-country mountain cyclist who races for the the SCOTT-SRAM MTB Racing Team. He won the world championship in men's cross-country biking in 2009, 2012, 2013, 2015, 2016, 2017, 2018, 2019, 2021 and 2022. He also won the gold medal in mountain biking at the 2016 Olympics, the silver medal 2012 Olympics and the bronze medal at the 2008 Olympics.
Emily Batty

Emily Batty is a Canadian cross-country mountain biker. She won a bronze medal at the 2016 World Championships. She is a 2 time Olympian and 2 time World Champ Pan-Am National Champion.
Catharine Pendrel

Catharine Pendrel is a Canadian cross-country mountain-biker. Pendrel was the world champion in cross-country mountain biking in 2011 and 2014 and the 2007 Pan American Games champion. She is also a bronze medalist in cross country Mountain biking.
Julien Absalon

Julien Arnaud Absalon is a French cross-country mountain biker. He is a 2 time Olympic Champion and 5 time world champion in mountain biking.
