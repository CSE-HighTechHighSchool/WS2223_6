Anatomy of a Mountain Bike


Handles

The handlebars are your main way to exert control over the bike. They are also home to the brake and shifter levers.

Wheel

Wheels are the most important part of the bicycle. The back wheel is powered by the pedals and they come in many different types and sizes.

Brakes

Brakes make you stop. Pushing the brake lever activates the pnuematics and compresses the brake caliper on the wheel, making it stop.

Seat

Seats (also known as saddles), are where you sit while riding. Make sure they're comfy because you'll be sitting there the whole ride.

Gears

Gears are important to master for good bikers. Changing gears changes your torque and speed ratios, and are controlled on the handlebars.

Frame

Frames are the skeleton of your bike. You can get basic steel or aluminum or expensive but light titanium or carbon fibre.

Pedals

Pedals are the mitochondria of the bike. Pedaling spins the back wheel. Make sure you are using gears properly to get the most out of your power.
