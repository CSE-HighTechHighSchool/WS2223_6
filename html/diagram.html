Anatomy of the Mountain Bike

Hover on one of the circles below for more information!

Handles

The handlebars are your main way to exert control over the bike. They are also home to the brake and shifter levers.

Wheel

Wheels are the most important part of the bicycle. The back wheel is powered by the pedals and they come in many different types and sizes.

Brakes

Brakes make you stop. Pushing the brake lever activates the pneumatics and compresses the brake caliper on the wheel, making it stop.

Seat

Seats (also known as saddles), are where you sit while riding. Make sure they're comfy because you'll be sitting there the whole ride!

Gears

Gears are important to master for good bikers. Changing gears changes your torque and speed ratios, and are controlled by a lever on the handlebars.

Frame

The frame is the skeleton of your bike. The frame can be made of cheap materials like aluminum or steel or they can be made out of expensive titanium or carbon fibre.

Pedals

Pedals are the powerhouse of the bike. Pedaling spins the back wheel. Make sure you are using gears properly to get the most out of your pedaling power!

Types of Mountain Bikes

Cross Country Bike

The cross country bike is made for flat paths, such as through forests or fields. It is a hardtail bike, meaning only the front wheel has suspension.

All Mountain Bike / Enduro Bike

All Mountain Bikes are made for a wide variety of trails, making them very versatile. The Enduro bike is similar, and is also better at jumps and descents.

Downhill

Downhill bikes are made for going down steep descents as fast as possible. It has a low seat, wide handlebars, and thick tires that get a good grip on the mountain.

Freeride

Freeride bikes are built for steep terrain and difficult obstacles. They can handle big drops and large jumps due to their robust components.

Fat Bikes

Fat bikes' main characteristic are their extremely thick wheels, which can be about 4-5 inches thick. They are built for riding on surfaces such as snow and sand, which require a lot of traction.
